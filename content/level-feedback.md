# End-of-Level Feedback Copy — Tier 1 (Levels 1–4)

All feedback follows the content guardrails: no shame, no "good/bad" food language, supportive and encouraging tone. Failure messages always frame the outcome as recoverable and offer a nudge toward improvement.

---

## Level 1: Carbohydrates

### Success

**Header:** "Fueled Up!"

**Message:**
> "Great work! You fueled up with carbs! Your muscles and brain now have the energy they need to keep going."

**Stats shown:**
- Carb items collected: X / Y
- Fibre-rich carbs found: X / Y
- Energy meter at finish: X%
- Knowledge Stars: 0–3

**Star summary:**
- Star 1: Collected all fibre-rich carbs
- Star 2: Found the hidden alcove
- Star 3: Finished with 90%+ energy

### Near-Miss (Level failed, but close — energy hit 0 in the last 25% of the level)

**Header:** "Almost There!"

**Message:**
> "You were so close! Your energy ran out just before the finish. Try collecting a few more carb items along the way — every one tops up your fuel."

**Hint:** "Fibre-rich carbs (like oats and fruits) give a little bonus energy. Look for the items with a green sparkle!"

### Fail (Energy hit 0 before the last 25%)

**Header:** "Running on Empty"

**Message:**
> "Your energy dipped to zero — time to refuel and try again! Focus on grabbing the carb items and steering clear of Fatigue Phantoms."

**Hint:** "Fatigue Phantoms glow before they appear. When you see the shimmer, get ready to jump!"

## Copy Style Guide (For Future Tiers)

These patterns should be followed when writing feedback for Tiers 2–4:

1. **Success headers** are 2–3 word punchy phrases tied to the level's concept.
2. **Success messages** always reinforce the core nutrition fact in one sentence, then add a "why it matters" sentence.
3. **Near-miss messages** are empathetic ("You were so close!") and immediately offer one actionable tip.
4. **Fail messages** never blame. They use language like "time to refuel," "another go," "grow and try again." They always offer a specific, actionable hint about either the mechanic or the enemy.
5. **Hints** are concrete and specific — they tell the player *what to look for* or *what to do differently*, not just "try harder."
6. **Stats** always show objective numbers — items collected, percentage, stars earned. No judgment language around the numbers.
